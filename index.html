About Me

I am a biomedical scientist in the US biopharma industry working in Alzheimer's Disease and Inflammatory Bowel Disease. Prior to my current role, I was a post-doctoral fellow with Dr. George Q. Daley, Dean of Harvard Medical School and Professor in the Stem Cell Program at Boston Children's Hospital.

My scientific interests encompass how cells receive, process, and respond to intrinsic and extrinsic signals, with particular focus on the fine-tuning of temporal and spatial control over gene expression through chromatin structure and function. I investigate how DNA and histone modifications, transcription factors, and signaling molecules collectively govern cell fates. This field is broadly defined as epigenetics.

Over the years, I have developed deep expertise in human genetics, genome-wide association studies, neuroimmunology, autoimmunity, and applications of machine learning in target discovery and validation. I deploy a diverse toolkit of model systems: human cell lines, iPSCs, mice, primary patient-derived cells, and organs-on-chips.

Beyond scientific R&D, I am actively engaged in the business of biopharma, including venture-backed company creation, venture capital, and business development.
